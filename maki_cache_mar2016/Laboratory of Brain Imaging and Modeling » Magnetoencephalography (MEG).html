Magnetoencephalography (MEG)


Magnetoencephalography (MEG) is a method of studying the human brain function non-invasively. Using super-conductive quantum interference devices (SQUIDs), MEG can simultaneously detect the extremely weak magnetic field elicited by spatially coherent neuronal currents. Different from functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), MEG provides direct sensitivity to the neuronal activity and has a millisecond temporal resolution. Using parallel MEG detection covering the whole head, anatomical information from magnetic resonance imaging, and mathematical constraints, the spatial distribution of MEG source signal can be estimated with approximately 5 millimeters.

Our lab is interested in developing MEG source modeling methods in order to further improve its spatial resolutions. Particularly we are interested in integrating MRI data to MEG analysis. We are also working on utilizing MEG’s high temporal resolution to study the spatio-spectral dynamics of neuronal activity of the human brain during tasks and at rest.
